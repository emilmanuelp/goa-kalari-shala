About the Academy

Rooted in Tradition.
Open to All.

Goa Kalari Shala was founded in 2018 by Aashan Bibin Kavumpurathu in the lush foothills of North Goa. Our mission is simple: to preserve the living tradition of Kalaripayattu and make it accessible to all — regardless of age, background, or prior experience.

Kalaripayattu (Kalari - training ground, Payattu - practice of arms) is over 3,000 years old, originating in the Malabar coast of Kerala. It is the mother of all Asian martial arts — a complete system of combat, healing, and self-development.

What We Teach

The Six Pillars

Kalaripayattu is not just combat — it is a complete science of human potential.

01

Meithari

Foundation body conditioning — intense flexibility, balance, and strength exercises drawn from yogic traditions. The root of all Kalari training.

02

Kolthari

Wooden weapon sequences — long staff (Kettukari), short stick (Cheruvadi), and curved stick (Ottakol). Develops timing, spatial awareness, and focus.

03

Ankathari

Metal weapon training — dagger, sword & shield, spear, and the legendary flexible sword (Urumi). Advanced students only, under direct Gurukkal guidance.

04

Verumkai

Empty-hand combat — the art of using the body itself as a weapon through strikes, locks, throws, and pressure-point techniques.

05

Kalari Chikitsa

The healing science — traditional massage and Ayurvedic therapy to restore the body after training injuries, improving strength and flexibility.

06

The Spiritual Path

Kalari is not merely combat — it is a journey inward. Every stance, every breath, every movement is a meditation. Students learn to still the mind, awaken inner energy (prana), and walk the ancient path of the warrior-sage: strength rooted in stillness, power guided by grace.

Meet the Gurukkal

Your Guides on the Path

Our instructors are not just teachers — they are lifelong practitioners of the art.

BK

Ashan Bibin Kavumpurathu

Chief Instructor & Founder

Ashan Bibin’s foundation is built upon the guidance of some of the most respected masters in the field. His expertise is rooted in a dual mastery of styles: Northern Style (Vadakkan): Disciplined under the esteemed Shri Shaji K. John Gurukkal at Spaces, Chennai. Southern Style (Thekkan): Trained under the venerable Shri E. S. Narayanan Embranthiri Gurukkal at the renowned ENS Kalari, Cochin. Beginning his formal path in 2010, Ashan Bibin spent years immersing himself in the physical, mental, and spiritual rigors of Kalari before transitioning into a teaching role in 2017. In 2018, Ashan Bibin brought his vision to the shores of Goa. Through a tireless series of workshops, regular classes, and private intensives spanning from Panjim and Salvador do Mundo to the vibrant hubs of Anjuna and Arambol, he cultivated a growing community of practitioners. His mission reached a historic milestone in November 2023 with the establishment of Goa Kalari Shala in Thivim. This marks the inauguration of Goa’s first traditional mud-floor Kalari Shala, a sacred space designed to honor the ancient architectural and energetic requirements of the practice.
